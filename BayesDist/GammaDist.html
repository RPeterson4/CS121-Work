The Gamma Distribution can be used to model the waiting time before the sth occurrence of an event. It can also be to model the expected number of occurrences of an event over an interval of time, similar to a poisson distribution. For \( X \), the Gamma Distribution is:

\( X \sim Gamma(s,r) \)

\( f(x|s,r) = \frac{r^2}{\Gamma (s)} x^{s-1} e^{-rx} \propto x^{s-1}e^{-rx} \)    for    \( x > 0 \) ,    \( s > 0 \) ,    and    \( r >0 \)

\( E(X|s,r) = \frac{s}{r} \)       and       \( Var(X|s,r) = \frac{s}{r^2} \)

Notice that both \( E(X|s,r) \) and \( Var(X|s,r) \) are directly related to \( s \) and inversely related to \( r \).

Here, the domain has been set from 0 to 100 while \( s \) ranges from 0 to 25 and \( r \) from 0 to 1.
