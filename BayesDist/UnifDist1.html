The Uniform Distribution is a continuous distribution along a range of x-values. For the random variable \(X\), the corresponding uniform density function \(f(x)\) is defined along a range in which every value is equally likely (hence the name uniform). As such, no distribution models uncertainty better than the uniform. The Uniform Distribution of \(X\) between a minimum and maximum x-value can be written as:

\(X \sim Unif(min,max)\)

whereby \(X\) has the corresponding probability density function:

\(f(x) = \frac{1}{max-min}\)    for    \(x\in(min,max)\)

Obviously, \(min \ngeq max\).

The mean and variance for \(X\) can be calculated as:

\(E(X)= \frac{1}{2}(a+b)\)       and       \(Var(X)=\frac{1}{12}(b-a)^2\)

Here, the x-axis, \(min\), and \(max\) each range from 0 to 1.
